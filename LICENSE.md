## Which Should I Choose?

**Choose Apache 2.0** if you want the strongest built-in patent protection. The patent grant and retaliation clause are part of the license itself (Section 3). This is recommended for commercial products and enterprise deployments.

**Choose MIT** if your organization has pre-approved MIT and you want the simplest possible license terms. You still receive patent protection from the separate PATENTS file and Defensive Patent Pledge.

**Either way,** you are free to use, modify, distribute, and build commercial products on the BUTL protocol. Both licenses are permissive. Neither requires you to open-source your own code.

## Why Dual License?

BUTL is a protocol that gains value through adoption. Dual licensing removes license-preference friction for both MIT-first and Apache-first organizations, following the same pattern used by Rust, serde, rand, and hundreds of other foundational open-source projects.

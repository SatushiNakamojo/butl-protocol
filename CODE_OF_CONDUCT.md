# Code of Conduct

## BUTL Protocol Community Standards

-----

## Our Purpose

The BUTL Protocol exists to give every person on the internet a trust layer that depends on math instead of middlemen. That mission requires a space where anyone can contribute, ask questions, disagree constructively, and build something meaningful.

This project may be maintained by only one person. These standards apply equally to the maintainer and to every contributor.

-----

## What Is Expected

**Respect.** Treat every person in this community the way you would want to be treated. Disagreements about technical approaches are welcome and valuable — they make the protocol better. Personal attacks are not welcome and never will be. Critique the idea, not the person.

**Clarity.** Write clearly. Explain your reasoning. When disagreeing, say why. When proposing something, describe the problem it solves. When asking a question, provide enough context for a meaningful answer. Clear communication respects everyone’s time — especially in a project where there may be only one person reading every issue and every pull request.

**Patience.** This project may have only one maintainer. Response times will vary. Sometimes replies come in hours, sometimes in days, occasionally longer. A delay should not be interpreted as disinterest. The same patience should be extended to other community members — not everyone has the same experience level, and the person asking a basic question today may be writing a reference implementation tomorrow.

**Honesty.** If something is unknown, say so. If a mistake was made, own it. If someone else’s approach is better, acknowledge it. Intellectual honesty is the foundation of good engineering.

**Good Faith.** Assume the best intentions behind other people’s words. Text communication strips away tone and body language. Read charitably before reacting.

-----

## What Will Not Be Tolerated

**Personal attacks.** Insulting, demeaning, or belittling another person — whether directed at their intelligence, their code, or their background. This includes passive-aggressive comments and condescension.

**Harassment.** Sustained unwelcome behavior directed at an individual, including repeated uninvited contact, threats, intimidation, or publishing someone’s private information without consent.

**Trolling.** Deliberately provocative or dishonest behavior intended to waste the community’s time rather than contribute to the project.

**Spam.** Using BUTL’s community channels to promote unrelated products or services.

**Plagiarism.** Claiming credit for someone else’s work or submitting contributions without the right to do so.

-----

## A Note on Technical Disagreements

This is a cryptographic protocol project. Technical disagreements are not only expected — they are essential. The protocol gets stronger when people challenge assumptions, question design decisions, and propose alternatives.

Technical disagreement is never a Code of Conduct violation. Saying “this approach is wrong because X, Y, Z” is exactly what this project needs. Saying “anyone who thinks this works is an idiot” is not.

The line is simple: argue about the work, not the person doing it.

Some of the best contributions to this project will come from people who disagree with the current design. That’s how protocols improve. Keep the arguments sharp and the respect intact.

-----

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all GitHub interactions (issues, pull requests, discussions, comments, reviews) and any future official BUTL communication channels.

It does not apply to personal matters, opinions on unrelated topics, or interactions in other communities.

-----

## Enforcement

### How to Report

If behavior that violates this Code of Conduct is experienced or witnessed:

**Option 1: Address it directly.** If it feels safe to do so, respond in the thread where the behavior occurred. Sometimes people don’t realize their tone has crossed a line, and a direct, respectful response resolves it.

**Option 2: Contact the maintainer privately.** Email satushinakamojo@protonmail.com with what happened, who was involved, and when/where it occurred. Include links or screenshots if available. Reports are treated confidentially.

### How Reports Are Handled

Since this project may have only one maintainer, all moderation may be handled by one person. Responses will be proportional to the behavior:

**Minor or first-time issues:** A private message explaining what was problematic and what is expected going forward. Most situations are resolved here.

**Repeated or moderate violations:** A public comment in the relevant thread making the standard clear, with a warning that continued behavior will result in further action.

**Severe or persistent violations:** Temporary or permanent ban from the project’s GitHub organization.

**Immediate ban (no warning):** Threats of violence, doxxing, or deliberate sabotage. These are non-negotiable.

### Disagreeing with a Decision

If a moderation decision is believed to be in error, an email to satushinakamojo@protonmail.com with an explanation of the disagreement is welcome. The decision will be reconsidered. However, the final decision on all moderation matters rests with the project maintainer.

-----

## Maintainer Commitments

Regardless of whether this project has one maintainer or many, the maintainer commits to:

- **Leading by example.** Holding themselves to the same standards asked of everyone else.
- **Reviewing with care.** A pull request represents someone’s time and effort. That effort is acknowledged even when changes are needed.
- **Being transparent.** When an issue is closed, a PR is rejected, or moderation action is taken, the reasoning will be explained.
- **Prioritizing the community.** If someone is consistently making this space worse — regardless of their technical ability — the community’s health comes first.
- **Being honest about limitations.** One person cannot do everything perfectly. Mistakes will be owned and corrected.
